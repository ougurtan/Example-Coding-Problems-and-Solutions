Hello and welcome to my programming practice set!

What is this course for?
This course will help you prepare for your Online Assessments (OAs) and software engineering (SWE) interviews using practice problems that closely mirror real internship and job interview questions. I will be uploading daily problems and their solutions, complete with detailed explanations.

You are the target demographic for this course if you are:

A student looking to get better at coding for your exams.

A job seeker wanting to improve your performance on OA questions.

Anyone just looking to sharpen their general programming skills.

What is this course NOT for?
This course is not meant to teach you C++ from scratch. If you are a complete beginner, I highly recommend checking out learncpp.com or the many excellent tutorials available on YouTube. This course focuses purely on making you better at C++ and sharpening your general programming logic. While you might be able to pick up C++ from this course alone, learncpp.com remains the community gold standard for foundational learning.

How to use this course
The questions in this set range from Medium to Hard difficulty—so don't get discouraged! I will include my own difficulty rating and the core topic at the top of every solution page.

If you are a beginner, I highly recommend starting from Day 1.

Under each day, you will find the main problem (or sometimes multiple numbered problems). To prevent accidental spoilers while scrolling, the corresponding solutions are kept in a separate file.

My recommended strategy:

Look at the problem and give yourself 30 minutes to try and solve it.

If you get stuck, open the solution file where I break down exactly why every single line of code is written the way it is.

If you don't get it right on your own, come back and retry the problem 3 to 4 days later. Repeat this cycle until you can solve it cleanly.

Pro Tip: Instead of skipping a day when you are short on time, just peek at the solution and focus on understanding the logic. This keeps your daily habit alive and ensures you still learn something new. Doing a little bit less than you planned is always better than doing nothing at all!

Good luck! :)
